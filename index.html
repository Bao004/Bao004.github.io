About

A senior CS major who likes systems that hold up under load — and the math that explains why.

I'm a Computer Science major with a minor in Mathematics, currently in my final year. My coursework and side projects sit at the intersection of two things I keep coming back to: building backend systems that are reliable and well-structured, and using data science to turn raw information into something a system — or a person — can act on.

The math minor isn't incidental. Probability, linear algebra, and optimization show up directly in how I think about model behavior and algorithmic tradeoffs, and I try to bring that same rigor to backend work: clear data models, predictable APIs, and systems designed with their failure modes in mind.
